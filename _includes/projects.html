Academic/Research Projects

SMART BIKE : Autonomous Self Driving bike with Perception

Robotics and Intelligent Systems Laboratory, ASU

• Designing the central control system of the Bike by using mechanical modelling and first principles under the guidance of Dr. Wenlong Zhang. Built a plant model with this information and designed a PID controller using controller tuning methods.

• Developing robot learning techniques on Human-Bike interactions and reducing bike operations cost and improving bike share logistics.

• Using SCANSE sweep LIDAR and Raspberry Pi V2 8MP 1080p camera to provide computer vision, object detection and navigation

Control of a Tip Tilt Platform for Parallelization of plates to study Thermal radiations

• Worked on control of a Tip-Tilt plus Z stage platform for parallelization of two plates. Designed an automated NI LabVIEW program to achieve control over the tip-tilt platform by switching between the actuators and ensuring robustness.

• Used NSC200 - NewPort controller to switch between manual and automatic modes. Interacted and Integrated with various instruments - Keithley 2000 , Tektronix Oscilloscope, Standa development kit.
Double Moon Classification problem using Multi Layer Perceptron

• Worked on creating a multilayer perceptron using artificial neural network techniques on the classic double moon classification problem for various distances between the moons.
Time Series prediction using Artificial Neural Computations and algorithms

• Forecasted future time data with limited data using a gradient descent algorithm in Neural networks using MATLAB.
Design and Modelling of a car cruise Control system using PC-104 and hardware-in-the-loop

• Obtained the differential equation of a car moving along a direction with opposing wind dynamics and gravity.
• Performed Hardware-in-Loop (HIL) testing using the PC104 and also via a secure server connection.

• Modelled and simulated the performance of the car with the help of Simulink and MATLAB for the linearized and Non-Linear models. Performed wind disturbance test for the speed control. Ongoing observations on stability.

• Ensured performance comparing the HIL results and checking for proper synchronization between hardware and software.
• Performed observability and controllability tests in MATLAB and checked for steady state outputs.
Master - Slave Robot (MS BOT): Thermal detection capable robots for human search operations

• Designed a stable and robust master slave robot configuration, fitted with thermal sensors using (LPC1700) ARM cortex M3 microprocessor with (SRF04) ultrasonic sensors for navigation to locate trapped humans in a disaster. Fitted the robot with cameras and GPS (PAM 7Q) to send the signal immediately to the control room in case of detection.

• Achieved almost 93% accuracy in trial runs with good detection ability and high speed of response.
Advanced Lighting Control system for a day-lit office

• Designed an efficient lighting control using photovoltaic PV sun meter to measure intensity sunlight and sending signal through a DAC to a controller which turns ON and OFF dimmable LEDs depending on the amount of light in the room.

• Able to set room lighting levels with minimum energy usage also considering the ambient lighting standards.
Border Navigation and Smart Tracking system with Alarm and GPS

• Project designed to help fishermen find the national borders of the country. A system built with an Arduino processor embedded with GPS, GSM and an alarm.

• Default national sea limits are specified in the microprocessor. Alarm system activated once, unit crosses the national boundaries. GSM unit fitted alongside, to alert the control room immediately.
